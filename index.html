Projects



LadderEDU. LadderEDU was originally developed at HackingEDU and after recieving good feedback from judges and possible investors, we continued to develop the app. Ladder is essentially a homework planning application with a powerful algorithmic backend. The app allocates time for each homework based on how much time you have and your grade in the class so that it can maximize your grade. It is available on iOS, Android, and Web. Check out the demo video > or, Check out the web-based application >

Glasses. Glasses is a location based social networking application based on the theory that each geographic location has a continually developing unique culture. Glasses allows you to look at any location's favorite movie, music, and hangout spot in order to view what is trending and locally popular. In addition to this, it allows one to post in a location, and others to reply to that post.

Tag. An application that I built because my mom asked me to. It allows users to tag locations (say, where they left their christmas lights) and then leads them back to where they left the tagged item. This works for items within a household, and is very accurate. While building this application, I ran into a lot of location accuracy problems, and I had to write an algorithm that toyed with iOS's CLLocationManager to get the best location accuracy.

CSSSA 2015. At California State Summer School for the Arts, I was exposed to a college-level, intensive, theater training program.
